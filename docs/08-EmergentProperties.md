## Emergent Properties

Properties that are strongly suggested by the ISA algebraic model. They emerge naturally from applying existing rules but are not axiomatically defined — their application is at the operator's discretion.

---

### P^N — Product Space

When multiplying States of different identity, P^N emerges from applying multiplication from each independent perspective. Each perspective generates exactly P instances (the product of all quantifiers). With N perspectives (one per distinct identity), the product space is P^N:

```
2(X) * 3(Y):
  From X:  2(X){ 6(Y) }  → 6 instances of Y
  From Y:  3(Y){ 6(X) }  → 6 instances of X
  Product space: 6 × 6 = 36 = 6²
```

```
2(X) * 3(Y) * 4(Z):
  From X:  2 × (3 × 4) = 24 instances
  From Y:  3 × (2 × 4) = 24 instances
  From Z:  4 × (2 × 3) = 24 instances
  Product space: 24 × 24 × 24 = 24³ = 13,824
```
